Quantum Computers

Quantum computers require superconductivity and to get that they must use multiple layers of cooling in order to get colder and colder.

As you get colder and colder you are able to access effects that wouldn't be possible without the cooling.

The superconductivity allows for zero resistance for electicity to flow.

Without being cooled you will get the quantum effects to decohear meaning you no longer have the desired quantum effects.

Quantum Bits

Bits are either 0 or 1 and can be represented by those two states. Qubits differ from bits in that they can be in a state of 0 1 or anthing inbetween. That is what makes them special because there is infintly many states between 0 and 1.
